About EPS Topik

The EPS or Employment Permit System is a program by the South Korean government that allows foreign workers from partner countries to work legally in Korea in industries such as manufacturing, construction, agriculture, and fisheries.

To work in Korea, you need to pass the EPS Topik exam, which tests your Korean language skills and understanding of Korean culture. The exam is conducted by the Human Resources Development Service of Korea (HRD Korea).

To prepare for the EPS Topik exam, you can take classes at Jcan Korean School, where we offer comprehensive training programs that cover all aspects of the exam.

Why work in Korea?

Through the EPS program, you can work in South Korea and earn around ₱80,000–₱100,000 or more per month, with higher income possible through overtime, night work, and holiday pay at 150% of the regular rate.

Working in Korea offers a unique opportunity to experience a different culture, improve your language skills, and gain valuable work experience in a global economy.

In addition, the EPS program provides a pathway to long-term employment and residency in Korea, making it an attractive option for many foreign workers.

The process

To become an EPS worker, first, you need to register and take the EPS-TOPIK Korean language test. Once you pass the exam, you can apply for job placement through the official EPS system.

After securing a job offer, you will need to complete the necessary paperwork and obtain a work visa. The EPS program also provides support for workers in Korea, including language training and cultural orientation.

At Jcan Korean School, we offer comprehensive training programs that cover all aspects of the EPS process, from language preparation to job placement assistance. Our experienced instructor will guide you through every step, ensuring that you are well-prepared for your journey to Korea.

Requirements

• Age: 18 to 38 years old

• No criminal record

• Peson with no forced detention/deportation

• Must have a valid passport

• Person with no disqualifying/restriction factors on leaving Philippines

• Person with no color blindness or color weakness

• Person with no existing or active EPS application

• Person who have no experience of staying in South Korea for 5 yrs or longer

• Person who are not diagnosed with infectious diseases as syphilis, tubercolosis, hepatitis, etc
